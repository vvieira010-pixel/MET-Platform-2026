CEFR Band Reference

Scores are based on Reading & Grammar (50 questions) and Listening (30 questions) — 80 total questions.

Level Range Classification Description
C1 68–80 Proficient user Can understand subtle meaning, complex detail, and more demanding texts across different genres and contexts.
B2 strong (target) 58–67 Strong independent user Can handle most B2 tasks with confidence and begin to manage more complex inference, global meaning, and multi-step evidence, but is not yet fully C1.
B2 40–57 Independent user Can understand main ideas, detail, and many inference questions, but may still miss subtle language or more complex connections.
B1 21–39 Starting independent user Can handle everyday language, simple details, and familiar topics, but still struggles with deeper inference and complex ideas.
A2 0–20 Basic user Can understand simple, familiar information and answer clear, direct questions.

How It Works

  • After completing all sections, the View Final CEFR Assessment button appears on the dashboard.
  • The score combines Reading & Grammar (50 Q) and Listening (30 Q) — 80 questions total.
  • Writing and Speaking are not automatically scored (they require human or AI evaluation).
  • The CEFR level is determined by the total correct answers across both sections.
  • A modal appears showing the level, description, score bar, and exact numbers.
