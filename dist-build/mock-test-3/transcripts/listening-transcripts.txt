--- Conversation C: Questions 27-30 ---

Narrator: Listen to a conversation about starting a business.

Man: I'm thinking of opening my own restaurant. I've been saving money for years.

Woman: That's exciting! But have you thought about how much it costs to start one?

Man: That's what I'm worried about. The rent for a good location is extremely high, and then there's equipment, staff, ingredients...

Man: You're right. I should focus on that before anything else.

--- Conversation D: Questions 31-33 ---

Narrator: Listen to a conversation about the environment.

Woman: I've been trying to reduce my plastic waste. I started bringing my own bags to the supermarket and using a reusable water bottle.

Man: That's good, but I feel like individual actions aren't enough to solve the bigger problem.

Woman: What do you mean?

Man: Well, large companies produce most of the pollution. Even if millions of people reduce their waste, it won't make as much difference as getting corporations to change their practices.

Woman: I see your point. But I think both are important. Individual actions set an example and create demand for change.

Man: That's true. I just wish governments would do more to regulate industries.

Narrator: Now answer questions 31, 32, and 33.
