SECTION B – SINGLE-TEXT READING

Passage 1: Urban Farms in the City
Over the last decade, urban farming has moved from a niche idea to a practical solution in many cities. Vacant lots, unused rooftops, and even old parking spaces are being transformed into productive gardens. These farms supply fresh vegetables to local residents, restaurants, and food banks, often at a lower cost than imported produce. Supporters say the farms improve access to healthy food, create small business opportunities, and reduce the distance food must travel before reaching consumers.

Urban farming also offers environmental benefits. Plants help cool hot city blocks, and green spaces can absorb rainwater that might otherwise overload drainage systems. In some neighborhoods, community gardens have become places where children learn how food grows and neighbors work together on shared projects. However, the movement is not without problems. Soil contamination, limited space, and high land prices can make it difficult to start or maintain a farm. In addition, some critics question whether urban farms can produce enough food to matter on a larger scale.

Despite these concerns, interest in urban farming continues to grow. City governments, nonprofit groups, and private businesses are investing in projects that support local agriculture. For many residents, the appeal is simple: they like the idea of eating food that was grown nearby and seeing unused land turned into something useful.

Text A
Library Notice: Exam Study Hours
The Northside Library will extend its evening study hours during the final exam period. From Monday through Friday, the building will remain open until 10:00 p.m. Students must bring a valid school ID to enter after 7:00 p.m. Quiet study rooms may be reserved at the front desk on a first-come, first-served basis. Food is not allowed in the study rooms, but drinks with lids are permitted.

Text B
Student Review
I used the library’s late hours last term while preparing for my final exams, and it made a big difference. The building was calm, the tables were usually available, and the quiet rooms helped me stay focused. My only complaint is that the study rooms filled up quickly on busy nights, so I had to arrive early if I wanted one.

Text C
Article: Why Quiet Study Spaces Matter
Students often underestimate how much their environment affects concentration. Research shows that quiet, predictable spaces can improve memory, reduce stress, and help learners manage long study sessions more effectively. Libraries are especially valuable during exam periods because they provide a structured place to work, away from distractions at home or in crowded cafés. Even when technology is available, many students still prefer a calm setting where they can concentrate without interruption.
